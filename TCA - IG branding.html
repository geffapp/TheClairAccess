A practical, execution-focused playbook to build, design, and scale The Clear Access Instagram presence. It combines modern longevity and aesthetic education with Thailand-focused guidance, using a calm, evidence-based visual identity and content system.

1 · Brand core & positioning

Who we are and how we speak

Brand essence

The Clear Access is a modern longevity and aesthetic advisory brand dedicated to helping foreigners coming to Thailand make safe, informed, and confident decisions about their health and beauty treatments.

Mission

To make longevity and aesthetic care accessible, understandable, and trustworthy by offering clear explanations, evidence-based guidance, and curated access to high-quality treatments and experts in Thailand.

Vision

To become the most trusted, modern, and unbiased source of clarity for anyone seeking longevity and aesthetic treatments in Thailand, setting a new standard in transparency, education, and personalised guidance.

Values

  • Clarity – simple, transparent, unbiased information.
  • Evidence – grounded in science and reliable data.
  • Calm authority – composed, precise, confident.
  • Independence – no hidden agenda, no sponsored hype.
  • Curation – only quality clinics, machines, and protocols.
  • Safety – reducing risk with education and realistic expectations.

Audience focus

Foreigners (Australia, Middle East, Europe), aged 25–55, who are planning or considering longevity or aesthetic procedures in Thailand and want neutral, trustworthy guidance instead of influencer noise.

Positioning statement
“The Clear Access provides modern, unbiased, science-led guidance to help people make informed, safe, and confident decisions about longevity and aesthetic treatments in Thailand.”

2 · Visual identity system

Colour, typography, and composition

2.1 Colour system

Three core colours define The Clear Access visual identity:

Navy HEX: #0D1B32
Usage: primary text, titles, key icons.
Gold HEX: #C1A577
Usage: accents, underlines, separators, tags.
Sand HEX: #E2D1BD
Usage: backgrounds, large surfaces, calm canvas.
Colour ratio guideline
Approximate target ratio per asset:
60% sand background · 30% navy text · 10% gold accents.

2.2 Typography

  • Titles / headings: modern serif (e.g. Cormorant Garamond, Playfair Display, or Georgia).
  • Body text: clean sans serif (e.g. Inter, Lato, or system UI font).
  • Numbers / technical labels: monospaced or semi-mono for clarity (optional).

Hierarchy (for Instagram assets):

  • Title: 32–48 pt equivalent (mobile first), always navy.
  • Subtitle: 18–24 pt, lighter weight, can use gold.
  • Body text: 16–20 pt, navy, generous line spacing (1.4–1.6).

2.3 Composition principles

  • Plenty of empty space; avoid crowding content.
  • Use gold as a thin line, tag, or subtle underline – never as full text block.
  • Avoid gradients and busy patterns; keep backgrounds calm.
  • Align text to left or centre, but keep consistent inside the same asset.

3 · Content architecture

Pillars & how they support traffic and leads

3.1 Core pillars

Pillar 1 – Thailand medical ecosystem

  • Explain why Thailand is strong in aesthetic and longevity treatments.
  • Highlight machines, accreditations, doctor training, and pricing logic.
  • Build trust and attract people researching “Thailand + treatment”.

Pillar 2 – Aesthetic treatment transparency

  • Explain key categories: HIFU, lasers, RF microneedling, fillers, threads, etc.
  • Use neutral comparisons (e.g. Ulthera vs generic HIFU).
  • Drive saves and shares – strongest for education and SEO inside IG.

Pillar 3 – Longevity education

  • Foundations: sleep, nutrition, metabolic health, strength, biomarkers.
  • Connect skin, body, and ageing into one clear story.
  • Drive long-term loyalty and interest in consultations & newsletters.

Supporting pillars

  • Myth-busting.
  • Beginner guides (“Your first Botox in Bangkok”).
  • Risk education (“Red flags in clinics”).
  • Travel + wellness tips.

3.2 Weekly content mix (baseline)

  • 4–5 main feed posts per week.
  • 2 carousels (education & transparency).
  • 2 reels (Thailand positioning + myth-busting / machines).
  • 1 infographic or static educational visual.
  • Stories almost daily.
Format Pros Cons
Carousels High saves, deep education, perfect for expert positioning. More work to design; risk of being too dense if text-heavy.
Reels (short) Strong reach and growth; ideal for top-of-funnel traffic. Lower depth; may need additional content to convert.
Reels (educational) Balances authority + reach; good for machine explainers. Needs scripts, clear structure, and more production effort.
Infographics Great for shares and quick value; easy to systematise. Can feel generic if not backed by real insights or references.

4 · Format rules – Reels

Sizes, safe zones, and text layout
4.1 Reel basics
Reels · 9:16 · traffic & awareness
  • Aspect ratio: 9:16.
  • Recommended size: 1080 × 1920 px.
  • Safe zones: keep text away from top/bottom 240 px to avoid UI overlap.
  • Ideal length (traffic): 4–8 seconds (hook reels) and 12–20 seconds (educational reels).
4.2 Reel hook structure (for traffic)

Guideline: first 0.3–0.8 seconds must clearly state the “why care” in big, readable text.

  • Use 1 short line, max 6–8 words.
  • Place hook text in upper-middle area.
  • Use navy text on sand background, with a thin gold underline if needed.
+----------------------------+ | "Coming to Thailand | | for Ulthera?" | | | | 3 things you need to | | check before booking. | | | | [The Clear | | Access tag] | +----------------------------+
  • Background: sand (#E2D1BD) full frame.
  • Hook text: 32–40 pt equivalent, navy (#0D1B32).
  • Highlight words with subtle gold underline if desired.
  • Subline: 20–24 pt, navy, 1–2 lines maximum.
4.3 Reel types for The Clear Access
  • Thailand positioning reels.
  • Machine explainer reels.
  • Myth-busting reels.
4.4 Reel text & captions
  • On-screen text: max 20–30 words for short reels.
  • Main caption: 2–4 short paragraphs, 1 key CTA.
  • Caption hook should mirror or complement on-screen hook.

4 · Format rules – Carousels

Education & saves
4.5 Carousel basics
Carousels · 4:5 · expert depth
  • Aspect ratio: 4:5 portrait.
  • Recommended size: 1080 × 1350 px.
  • Slide count: 5–8 slides for education; 3–5 for simple myth-busting.
  • Text density: 40–70 words per slide, maximum.
4.6 First slide – traffic & clarity

The first slide must answer “What is this about?” in one glance.

+--------------------------------+ | Ulthera vs generic HIFU | | in Bangkok | | | | What actually changes and | | what does not. | | | | [TCA logo/tag] | +--------------------------------+
  • Title: 32–40 pt, navy, 1–2 lines.
  • Subtitle: 18–22 pt, navy, 1–2 lines.
  • Background: sand, clear top and middle area.
  • Optional micro-tag: small gold bar or “Thailand · Aesthetics” tag.
4.7 Internal slides – structure
  1. Slide 1: Title + context.
  2. Slide 2: “What it is”.
  3. Slide 3: “Why it matters”.
  4. Slide 4: “Risks / limits”.
  5. Slide 5: “Thailand angle”.
  6. Slide 6–7: “Checklist / questions to ask”.
  7. Slide 8: CTA (“Save this before your consultation”).
4.8 Layout guidelines
  • Use consistent margins (e.g. 80 px left/right).
  • Limit each slide to 3–5 bullets or 2 short paragraphs.
  • Use navy for body text; gold only for highlights and lines.
  • Keep diagrams extremely simple.

4 · Format rules – Stories

Micro content & funnel support
4.9 Stories basics
Stories · 9:16 · daily touchpoints
  • Aspect ratio: 9:16, 1080 × 1920 px.
  • Ideal use: 1–5 stories per day when active.
  • Use for: polls, micro myth-busting, Q&A, preparation tips.
4.10 Story layout patterns
  • Single fact with one short sentence.
  • Question at top + poll sticker in the middle.
  • Before/after state (“Confused → Clear”).
